The Unique Challenges of High-Value Patent Transactions

Transactions involving Standard Essential Patents (SEPs) and litigation-grade patents are fundamentally different from ordinary patent sales. Their complexity and sensitivity make traditional public brokerage models unsuitable.

Challenges for Asset Holders (Sellers)

  • The Dilemma of Public Disclosure
    High-value patents are often core strategic assets. Sellers usually cannot publicly announce their intent to sell, making it difficult for potential buyers to discover them and leading to missed opportunities.
  • Risk of a Prolonged Transaction Cycle
    These transactions can take months or even years. Without a professional framework, deals often collapse due to high communication costs and information asymmetry.
  • High Cost of Building Trust
    Buyers and sellers are often in the same industryâpotential partners but also competitors. Direct negotiations can create strategic concerns and undermine trust from the start.

Challenges for Acquirers (Buyers)

  • Difficulty in Discovering Quality Assets
    Truly valuable patent assets are rarely sold on the open market. Buyers struggle to find high-quality targets that align with their strategic needs through conventional channels.
  • Complexity of Due Diligence
    Valuing SEPs and litigation-grade patents is extremely complex, requiring deep technical and legal expertise. Most buyers cannot independently conduct a comprehensive risk assessment.
  • Risk of Exposing Strategic Intent
    Directly contacting a seller reveals your own technological roadmap and strategic direction. This information could be exploited by competitors or the seller, damaging your long-term advantage.
